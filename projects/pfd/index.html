Probability-Flow Distillation: Exact Wasserstein Gradient Flow for High-Fidelity 3D Generation

Rohith Ramanan1, A. N. Rajagopalan1
1Indian Institute of Technology Madras
arXiv Code (coming soon)

Qualitative results of Probability-Flow Distillation on text-to-3D generation, compared against SDS baselines.

Abstract

Score distillation sampling (SDS) has enabled text-to-3D generation by distilling 2D diffusion priors into neural radiance fields, but suffers from well-known artifacts including over-saturation and geometric inconsistency.

We show that these failure modes stem from an inexact approximation of the Wasserstein gradient flow in the distillation objective. Probability-Flow Distillation (PFD) corrects this by deriving an analytically exact gradient flow in probability space, recovering the true transport direction at each distillation step.

The result is a simple, drop-in replacement for SDS that produces sharper geometry, richer texture, and improved multi-view consistency — without additional training or inference overhead.

Method

Standard SDS computes gradients by treating the diffusion denoiser as a fixed critic — equivalent to an approximate Wasserstein gradient step that accumulates error over the distillation trajectory. PFD solves the exact continuity equation at each step, yielding a provably convergent flow toward the data manifold.
